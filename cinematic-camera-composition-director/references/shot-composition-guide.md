# Shot Composition Guide

Use this only when exact shot vocabulary or composition choices are needed.

## Viewpoint

- Objective: the audience observes from outside the character.
- Subjective: the frame bends toward a character's emotional state.
- POV: the camera becomes what the character sees.

## Shot Size

- Extreme long shot: location, isolation, scale, vulnerability.
- Long shot: full body and environment.
- Medium shot: behavior, dialogue, social tension.
- Cowboy shot: body readiness, confrontation, hands/props.
- Close-up: inner pressure, decision, recognition.
- Extreme close-up: obsession, detail, threat, sensory intensity.

## Subject Angle

- Front: direct confrontation or emotional openness.
- Profile: distance, thought, observation, divided attention.
- Three-quarter: natural conversation, balanced depth.
- Back angle: secrecy, alienation, pursuit, withheld identity.

## Camera Height

- Eye level: parity, realism, neutrality.
- Low angle: power, menace, awe, instability if extreme.
- High angle: vulnerability, exposure, judgment, fragility.
- Overhead: pattern, fate, surveillance, abstraction.
- Ground level: danger, impact, physical texture.

## Composition Tools

- Rule of thirds: readable balance and controlled emphasis.
- Center framing: ritual, confrontation, symmetry, deadpan, power.
- Leading lines: direct attention and imply movement.
- Negative space: loneliness, threat, anticipation, imbalance.
- Foreground/midground/background: depth, secrecy, surveillance, layered
  conflict.
- Frame within frame: entrapment, observation, distance.
- Screen direction: preserve left-to-right or right-to-left movement unless
  confusion is intended.

## Lens Feeling

- Wide lens: space, pressure, distortion, physical proximity.
- Normal lens: natural perspective, grounded realism.
- Telephoto: compression, surveillance, emotional distance, crowd pressure.
- Shallow depth: isolation, intimacy, distraction removal.
- Deep focus: social context, simultaneity, moral complexity.

## Movement

Use movement as a dramatic verb:

- Push in: realization, threat, commitment.
- Pull back: loss, revelation, loneliness.
- Track: pursuit, discovery, momentum.
- Pan: attention shift, reveal, surveillance.
- Handheld: instability, urgency, embodied tension.
- Locked off: control, dread, observation, ritual.
