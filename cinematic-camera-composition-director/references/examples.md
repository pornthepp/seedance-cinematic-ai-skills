# Camera Composition Examples

Worked examples showing input and full output.

## Example 1: Vague Request To Precise Shot

### User Input

> I need a shot of a woman sitting alone in a diner at night. Make it cinematic.

### Output

**Dramatic purpose:** Isolation and quiet desperation. The audience should feel
the weight of her being the only person awake in a sleeping city.

**Viewpoint:** Objective. The audience watches from outside her world.

**Shot size:** Wide shot through the diner window. Her body is small inside the
frame, surrounded by empty booths and fluorescent light.

**Camera height and angle:** Eye level, slightly below the window frame. The
glass creates a natural frame-within-frame. The audience is outside, looking in.

**Lens feeling:** 35mm. Wide enough to show the empty diner around her, normal
enough to keep perspective grounded. No distortion.

**Composition:** She sits frame right at the counter, occupying one-third of the
image. The left two-thirds are empty stools, napkin holders, a pie case with one
slice left. Leading lines from the counter converge toward her. Negative space
dominates.

**Lighting:** Overhead fluorescent inside the diner creates a cold, flat wash on
her face. Outside is dark blue. The window reflects fragments of the street. A
single neon sign provides an accent of warm red or amber on the glass surface.

**Movement:** Locked off. No movement. The stillness is the point.

**Continuity note:** If this opens a sequence, the next shot should cut closer —
medium close-up from inside the diner — to bring the audience across the glass
barrier and into her space.

**Final image prompt:**

```text
Wide shot through a diner window at night. A woman in her 30s sits alone at the counter, frame right, hunched slightly over a coffee cup. The diner is empty — row of vacant stools, napkin holders, a glass pie case with one remaining slice. Overhead fluorescent lights cast a flat, cold wash. Outside is dark blue-black. A neon sign reflects warm amber on the window glass. The woman's face is partially reflected in the window. 35mm lens feeling, eye level, locked camera, shallow depth on the window surface. The composition emphasizes how much empty space surrounds one person.
```

## Example 2: Shot Sequence For Emotional Turn

### User Input

> A father finds out his teenage daughter has been lying to him. They're in the
> kitchen. I need 3 shots that show the power shift — he starts in control, then
> she takes over.

### Output

**Shot 1 — Discovery**

| Element      | Choice                                                                                                       |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Beat         | Father reads a message on her phone. His expression hardens.                                                 |
| Viewpoint    | Objective                                                                                                    |
| Shot size    | Medium close-up on father                                                                                    |
| Height/angle | Slightly low angle. He still holds power.                                                                    |
| Lens         | 50mm. Neutral, grounded.                                                                                     |
| Composition  | Father fills frame left. The kitchen behind him is orderly — his domain. Shallow depth blurs the background. |
| Movement     | Slow push in as he reads. 2-3 inches, barely perceptible.                                                    |
| Continuity   | His right hand holds the phone. Kitchen window behind him shows late afternoon light.                        |

**Shot 2 — Confrontation**

| Element      | Choice                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| ------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Beat         | He puts down the phone and looks at her. She freezes mid-action.                                                                                                                                     |
| Viewpoint    | Objective, shifting toward her subjective state                                                                                                                                                      |
| Shot size    | Two-shot, medium. Both visible. Kitchen island between them — a barrier.                                                                                                                             |
| Height/angle | Eye level. Power is contested.                                                                                                                                                                       |
| Lens         | 35mm. Shows the space between them. The kitchen island reads as a wall.                                                                                                                              |
| Composition  | Father frame left, daughter frame right. The island cuts the frame in half. Knife block, cutting board, and fruit bowl on the island create a domestic still life between two people about to fight. |
| Movement     | Locked off. Stillness creates tension.                                                                                                                                                               |
| Continuity   | Same late afternoon light. His hand is now flat on the counter.                                                                                                                                      |

**Shot 3 — Power Shift**

| Element      | Choice                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| ------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Beat         | She does not apologize. She says something that exposes his own hypocrisy. He has no answer.                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Viewpoint    | Shifting to her subjective dominance                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Shot size    | Close-up on father, then cut to medium close-up on daughter                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Height/angle | Father is now shot from slightly high angle — he has lost authority. Daughter is shot from slightly low angle — she has taken control.                                                                                                                                      |
| Lens         | 85mm on father's close-up. Compression isolates him. 50mm on daughter — she is grounded and clear.                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Composition  | Father close-up: he is frame center but his eyes drop. The background is now soft and undefined — his control of the kitchen space has dissolved. Daughter medium close-up: she stands straighter, frame center, the kitchen window behind her catches the last warm light. |
| Movement     | Static on both. The cut is the movement.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Continuity   | Light has shifted slightly warmer as the scene ends. His hand has moved off the counter.                                                                                                                                                                                    |

**Sequence logic:** The camera starts favoring the father (low angle, medium
close-up, orderly background), passes through neutral territory (eye-level
two-shot with a barrier), and ends favoring the daughter (she gets the low
angle, the warm light, the grounded lens). Power is transferred through camera
height, lens choice, and background treatment — not through dialogue alone.

## Example 3: Correcting A Bad Shot Description

### User Input

> My current shot description says: "Epic cinematic wide angle drone shot of the
> hero walking dramatically toward camera with golden hour backlighting, 8K,
> ultra detailed." Fix it.

### Output

**Problems with the original:**

1. "Epic cinematic" — decorative, not a camera instruction.
2. "Wide angle drone" — wide angle and drone are two separate decisions. Why
   drone? What story purpose?
3. "Walking dramatically" — not filmable. What does the walk look like?
4. "Golden hour backlighting" — acceptable but needs specifics: sun position,
   shadow direction, fill level.
5. "8K, ultra detailed" — technical resolution specs do not change composition
   or emotion.

**Revised shot:**

```text
Low-angle wide shot from ground level. A man in a dust-covered coat walks toward camera along a straight dirt road. His stride is steady but heavy — boots dragging slightly, shoulders forward. Late afternoon sun sits directly behind him at 15 degrees above horizon, creating a long shadow stretching toward camera. Rim light outlines his silhouette and catches dust particles in the air. His face is underexposed, just readable. The road stretches to a vanishing point behind him. Flat farmland on both sides, no buildings. 24mm lens feeling — the road appears to stretch forever. Locked camera, no movement. The man grows larger in frame as he approaches.
```

**What changed:** Every vague word became a visible instruction. The camera has
a reason (ground level = his weight and exhaustion read through the walk). The
lens has a reason (24mm stretches the road to emphasize distance traveled). The
lighting has physics (sun position, shadow direction, rim light, underexposed
face). The performance has behavior (heavy stride, dragging boots, shoulders
forward) instead of "dramatically."
