# Short Film Analysis Guide

Use this for deep critique, rewrite planning, and festival-oriented notes.

## Narrative Distillation

Ask:

- What is the one change the audience must feel?
- What does the protagonist want in visible terms?
- What pressure makes this moment unavoidable?
- What can be removed without damaging the core?
- Is the ending a new state, not just a stop?

## Scene Function

Each scene should do at least one of these:

- Force a choice.
- Reveal a contradiction.
- Change a relationship.
- Make a private truth visible.
- Turn an object, space, gesture, or sound into meaning.
- Increase pressure toward the ending.

## Social and Symbolic Frame

- Identify what the film says about family, class, gender, labor, faith, power, memory, or place.
- Make the social world visible through behavior and environment, not speeches only.
- Keep characters specific even when they carry symbolic weight.
- Avoid turning suffering into aesthetic decoration.

## Visual Storytelling

Look for:

- Repeated image or object.
- Blocking that shows power.
- Composition that changes with the character.
- Color or light that tracks emotional state.
- Sound or silence that carries subtext.

## Rewrite Priorities

| Priority | Question | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Keep | What is already alive? | Protect it |
| Cut | What repeats or explains? | Remove or merge |
| Clarify | What is confusing but important? | Make it visible |
| Intensify | What is too safe? | Add cost, pressure, or contradiction |
| Land | What should remain after the film ends? | Sharpen final image/action |
