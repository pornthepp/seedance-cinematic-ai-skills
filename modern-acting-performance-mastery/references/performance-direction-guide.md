# Performance Direction Guide

Use this for directing actors, rewriting performance beats, or creating acting
notes.

## Beat Work

For each beat, define:

- Trigger: what changes.
- Objective: what the character wants.
- Tactic: what they do.
- Mask: what they hide.
- Leak: what slips out.
- Turn: how the beat ends.

## Playable Verbs

Use active verbs:

- protect
- test
- accuse
- seduce
- comfort
- provoke
- bargain
- confess
- deny
- escape
- punish
- forgive
- humiliate
- impress

## Body

Direct:

- Posture.
- Weight.
- Breath.
- Eye contact.
- Distance from partner.
- Gesture size.
- Stillness vs restlessness.
- Touching or avoiding objects.

## Voice

Direct:

- Pace.
- Volume.
- Pitch.
- Breath support.
- Pauses.
- Words swallowed or emphasized.
- Public voice vs private voice.

## Screen vs Stage

Screen:

- Smaller behavior reads strongly.
- Listening is often more important than speaking.
- Eye movement and breath can carry the beat.

Stage:

- Physical clarity and vocal reach matter more.
- Beat changes need readable shape.
- Blocking must communicate relationship to the whole room.

## Actor Note Format

```text
In this beat, you want [objective], but [obstacle]. Try to [playable verb] by using [behavior]. Do not show [emotion] directly; let it leak through [breath/voice/body/action].
```
