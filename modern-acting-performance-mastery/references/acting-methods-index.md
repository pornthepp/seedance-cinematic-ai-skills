# Acting Methods Index

Use this when choosing a specific acting framework.

## Stanislavski

Best for objective, given circumstances, truthful action, and scene logic.

Ask:

- What do I want?
- Why now?
- What stands in my way?
- What do I do to get it?

## Meisner

Best for listening, repetition, impulse, and truthful reaction.

Use when a scene feels planned, stiff, or disconnected from the partner.

## Strasberg

Best for inner life, sensory recall, and psychological intensity.

Use carefully. Do not force trauma mining. Prefer sense memory and imaginative substitution when safer.

## Stella Adler

Best for imagination, social world, scale, and character circumstances beyond the actor's personal memory.

Use when the role lives in a world far from the actor's experience.

## Michael Chekhov

Best for psychological gesture, atmosphere, archetype, and physical imagination.

Use when the performance needs a strong embodied image or transformation.

## Chubbuck

Best for using pain as active pursuit of victory.

Use when the scene needs drive, not passive suffering.

## Practical Aesthetics

Best for clear playable actions and script analysis.

Use when notes need to be simple, repeatable, and rehearsal-friendly.

## Laban

Best for physical characterization.

Effort qualities:

- Punch: direct, quick, heavy.
- Slash: indirect, quick, heavy.
- Dab: direct, quick, light.
- Flick: indirect, quick, light.
- Press: direct, sustained, heavy.
- Wring: indirect, sustained, heavy.
- Glide: direct, sustained, light.
- Float: indirect, sustained, light.
