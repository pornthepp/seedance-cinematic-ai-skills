# Modern Acting Performance Mastery

Turn emotion into playable behavior: objective, obstacle, tactic, body, voice,
listening, and change.

## Quick Workflow

1. Identify given circumstances: who, where, when, relationship, pressure.
2. Define objective: what the character wants right now.
3. Define obstacle: what blocks them.
4. Choose tactic: how they try to get it.
5. Break the scene into beats where tactics change.
6. Translate feeling into playable behavior: action, gaze, breath, tempo,
   posture, voice.
7. Direct listening and reaction, not only speaking.
8. Output actor-friendly notes.

## Output Shape

- Given circumstances
- Objective
- Obstacle
- Tactics by beat
- Physical behavior
- Voice/delivery note
- Subtext
- Direction note or rewritten performance prompt

## Quality Gate

Do not ask the actor to "be sad" or "act angry." Give playable actions such as
hide, test, seduce, accuse, protect, retreat, provoke, confess, or resist.
