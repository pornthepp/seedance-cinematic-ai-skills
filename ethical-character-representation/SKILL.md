# Ethical Character Representation

Improve character design by checking agency, specificity, social framing,
cultural signals, and narrative role.

## Token Discipline

Start here. Read one reference only when needed:

| Need                                                                        | Read                                        |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Full audit methodology, failure modes, concrete redesign options            | `references/representation-review-guide.md` |

## Quick Workflow

1. Identify the character's story role, desire, and dramatic function.
2. List identity signals: body, costume, culture, accent, age, gender, class,
   ability, status, symbols, and environment.
3. Check agency: desire, choice, competence, contradiction, and consequences.
4. Check social frame: who gets power, humor, beauty, violence, vulnerability,
   and complexity.
5. Check visual framing: silhouette, costume, lighting, camera treatment, props,
   and body language.
6. Review pattern risk across the cast or world, not just one character.
7. Suggest concrete upgrades in story, silhouette, costume, dialogue,
   relationships, and world context.

## Output Shape

Read `templates/character-review.md` first. Fill in each row:

- `Agency` — desire, choice, competence, contradiction, consequences
- `Visual design` — silhouette, costume, lighting treatment, body language
- `Cultural specificity` — signals, symbols, accuracy, and what is missing
- `Narrative role` — function in story and whether it reduces or expands humanity
- `Social framing` — who gets power, beauty, humor, violence, complexity
- `Cast/world pattern` — how this character sits in the ensemble or world

## Quality Gate

The result should be more specific, more cinematic, and more human, without
turning identity into decoration, a joke, a single trauma, or a moral lesson.
