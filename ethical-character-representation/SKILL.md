# Ethical Character Representation

Improve character design by checking agency, specificity, social framing, cultural signals, and narrative role.

## Quick Workflow

1. Identify the character's story role, desire, and dramatic function.
2. List identity signals: body, costume, culture, accent, age, gender, class, ability, status, symbols, and environment.
3. Check agency: desire, choice, competence, contradiction, and consequences.
4. Check social frame: who gets power, humor, beauty, violence, vulnerability, and complexity.
5. Check visual framing: silhouette, costume, lighting, camera treatment, props, and body language.
6. Review pattern risk across the cast or world, not just one character.
7. Suggest concrete upgrades in story, silhouette, costume, dialogue, relationships, and world context.

## Quality Gate

The result should be more specific, more cinematic, and more human, without turning identity into decoration, a joke, a single trauma, or a moral lesson.
