# Soundscape Guide

Use this for cue planning, sound palettes, and deeper audio review.

## Sound Functions

- Support: reinforce the visible emotion.
- Contrast: create irony, dread, or emotional complexity.
- Reveal: expose hidden memory, danger, or desire.
- Withhold: use silence or thin sound to create pressure.
- Bridge: carry emotion across a cut.
- Disrupt: mark rupture, trauma, shock, or realization.

## Palette Elements

- Music: harmony, melody, rhythm, orchestration, motif.
- Ambience: room tone, weather, city, nature, mechanical beds.
- Foley: cloth, breath, footsteps, props, body contact.
- Designed sound: drones, impacts, processed textures, subjective sound.
- Voice: dialogue, whisper, breath, chant, crowd, memory.
- Silence: absence used as pressure, focus, or moral weight.

## Motif Rules

- Give a motif a clear dramatic owner: person, place, wound, desire, threat.
- Vary motif by harmony, tempo, instrument, register, or absence.
- Do not state the motif every time the owner appears.
- Let the motif fail, distort, or disappear when the story changes.

## Mix Notes

- Foreground only one or two elements at a time.
- Preserve dialogue clarity unless confusion is intentional.
- Use low frequency sparingly for dread or scale.
- Let reverb describe space and psychology.
- Match cuts with sonic continuity when the image jumps.
