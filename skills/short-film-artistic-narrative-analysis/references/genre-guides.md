# Genre Guides

Use genre guidance to choose priorities, not to add empty style words.
Every genre still needs a subject, one visible beat, camera logic,
physical light, audio intent, and constraints.

## Product Ad

Preserve product identity above all. Use one reveal, clean reflections,
material-specific light, and controlled camera movement. Put logo, label,
shape, and material constraints near the start, especially in I2V or R2V.
Prefer moving light, mist, condensation, particles, or camera around the
product rather than transforming the product itself.

**Key moves:** slow orbit, push-in to detail, light sweep across surface,
condensation forming, package rotation with catch light.

## Short Drama

Use gesture, blocking, silence, and a visible emotional turn. Keep the
prompt anchored to one beat: the character enters, notices, decides,
hides, reveals, or leaves. Assign each character by tag and keep dialogue
short. Use locked or slow push-in camera when facial performance matters.

**Key moves:** lingering close-up, over-shoulder reaction, wide
establishing shot with character entering, silence before the turn.

## Action

Use one objective, one obstacle, one camera move, and explicit character
tags. Specify force, timing, and consequence: who ducks, who lands,
what breaks, what remains intact. If hands, faces, or props matter,
reduce action complexity or split into clips.

**Key moves:** tracking shot following movement, whip pan to impact,
low-angle power shot, handheld urgency, slow-motion on the key hit.

## Animation / 2D

Use medium, line quality, palette, deformation, timing, texture, and
motion rhythm. Avoid named studio, franchise, or artist labels.
Describe original character shapes, not references to existing IP.

**Key moves:** held frame with eye blink, sakuga burst for action,
impact frame with speed lines, background scroll, limited animation
with expressive timing.

## Horror

Use restraint over spectacle. What is not shown creates more tension
than what is shown. Build with environmental sound, slow camera,
shadow movement, and delayed reveals.

**Key moves:** slow dolly through empty space, static wide shot with
movement in one corner, close-up on face reacting to off-screen sound,
light source flickering, long silence broken by one sharp sound.

## Documentary

Use naturalistic framing, observational camera, available light
language, and room tone. Avoid overly composed or stylized setups.
The camera watches rather than directs.

**Key moves:** handheld following subject, static wide establishing,
medium shot interview framing, b-roll detail shots, natural light
transitions.

## Music Video

Use rhythm-driven editing, bold color, and performance energy.
Sync visual beats to audio beats. Allow more stylistic freedom
but keep each shot's action clear.

**Key moves:** beat-synced cuts, slow-motion on chorus, whip pan
transitions, color shift between sections, performance close-up
with locked camera.

## Commercial

Use clean compositions, aspirational but grounded visuals, and
product integration that feels natural. End on the product or
brand moment.

**Key moves:** lifestyle establishing shot, product in-use moment,
detail close-up, hero shot with clean background, logo/tagline
endframe.
