# Look Development Guide

Use this when color needs more than a quick palette.

## Palette Logic

- Realist: color follows location, practical lights, weather, and production
  design.
- Expressionist: color reveals emotion more than literal realism.
- Symbolic: color carries theme, memory, guilt, desire, danger, or belonging.
- Arc-based: color changes as the character or story changes.

## Palette System

Define:

- Base colors: the visual world the audience accepts as normal.
- Character colors: repeated associations for a person, faction, or desire.
- Accent colors: small high-signal colors used for clues, rupture, or focus.
- Forbidden colors: colors avoided until a major turn.
- Transition colors: bridge colors used when the emotional state changes.

## DOP and Colorist Notes

- Shoot with enough exposure latitude for the planned grade.
- Keep skin tone intentional even when the world is stylized.
- Match practical light color to story logic.
- Separate production design color from post-production color.
- Name what should remain neutral.

## Grading Layers

1. Exposure and contrast.
2. White balance and color temperature.
3. Saturation and hue bias.
4. Skin isolation if needed.
5. Highlight and shadow color.
6. Grain, halation, bloom, film emulation, or texture.
7. Shot matching and scene continuity.
