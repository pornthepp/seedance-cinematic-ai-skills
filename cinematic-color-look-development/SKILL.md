# Cinematic Color Look Development

Build color from story, emotion, lighting, skin tone, and continuity.

## Token Discipline

Start here. Read one reference only when needed:

## Quick Workflow

1. Identify story emotion and transformation.
2. Define dominant palette, accent color, and what each color means.
3. Decide whether the color logic is realistic, expressive, symbolic, or
   arc-based.
4. Protect skin tone, exposure, and motivated light.
5. Plan the grade in layers: contrast, saturation, hue bias, highlight/shadow
   behavior, grain or film emulation.
6. Check continuity across shots or scenes.
7. Output a palette, look note, prompt, or review table.

## Output Shape

Read `templates/look-development-card.md` first. Fill in each field:

- `Emotional color intention` — what the color is doing for the story
- `Dominant palette` — primary hue family and saturation logic
- `Accent color` — the contrast signal and where it appears
- `Lighting logic` — motivated light source, direction, and color temp
- `Grade/LUT note` — contrast curve, hue bias, shadow/highlight behavior
- `Continuity risk` — how this look changes across cuts or scenes
- `Final prompt or production note` — assembled output

## Quality Gate

Color must clarify the scene, not decorate it. Avoid one-word looks like "teal
orange" unless the story reason, lighting method, and continuity plan are clear.
